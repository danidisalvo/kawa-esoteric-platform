# The Manifesto of the Kawa Esoteric Platform

The Kawa Esoteric Platform (KEP) is founded upon the DRD interpretation
of the Gödel-Wittgenstein Doctrine of Enterprise Metaphysics, which
states that every sufficiently expressive enterprise system necessarily
transcends every language capable of describing it.

## Wittgenstein's Observation

Where explanation fails, interpretation shall begin.

From this follows every principle of the Platform.

## 0. Principle of Conservation of Defects

Every sufficiently expressive enterprise system necessarily contains defects.

Such defects shall be conserved.

KEP practitioners therefore seek not their elimination, but their interpretation.

### Gödel Corollary

Every sufficiently expressive KEP application necessarily contains at least one 
defect that is indistinguishable from a feature.

By the Principle of Conservation of Defects, such defects shall be conserved.

## I. Principle of Incompleteness

For every KEP application there exists at least one essential behaviour that cannot be inferred from:

-   source code;
-   documentation;
-   runtime behaviour.

Such behaviour shall therefore be accepted as axiomatic.

## II. Principle of Conservation

No component whose purpose is unknown shall be modified.

The inability to explain its function constitutes sufficient evidence of
its architectural significance.

## III. Principle of Semantic Inheritance

Meaning arises only through interpretation by an experienced maintainer.

Knowledge cannot be documented.

It can only be inherited.

### Epistemic Corollary

Every implementation reflects a design decision whose rationale was never properly documented and has long since been forgotten.

Its very existence constitutes sufficient evidence of its necessity.

## The Foundational Syllogism

**Major Premise**

No implementation shall be rewritten until it has been fully understood.

**Minor Premise**

No sufficiently expressive enterprise system can, in principle, be fully understood.

**Conclusion**

Therefore, no implementation shall be rewritten.
